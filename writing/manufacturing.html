◉ The short answer

Nautilida runs the weekly rhythm so the team (leadership, AMs, channel managers) walks into Monday already knowing where to act. Built around the signals manufacturing revenue actually has: reorder patterns, RFQ thread state, channel-partner activity, spec discussions across engineering and procurement.

Long sales cycles. Multiple buyer-side stakeholders. Reorder rhythms that vary by account. RFQs scattered across email, CRM, and spec sheets. Channel partners drifting in and out of activity. Manufacturing revenue lives in the details. The weekly loop reads those details and routes them to the right owner.

The problem in manufacturing

What the team is actually fighting in this sector. The things that don't show up in a pipeline chart.

  1. Long sales cycles with multiple buyer-side stakeholders (engineering, procurement, plant ops) and no system that holds the full thread.
  2. Reorder rhythms vary by account: some monthly, some quarterly, some only when stock runs low. No easy way to see which ones are slipping until a quarter is already missed.
  3. RFQs and quote revisions scattered across email, CRM, and spec sheets, with no memory tying them to historical decisions on the same account.
  4. Channel partners and distributors that go quiet for weeks and nobody notices until pipeline is gone.
  5. AM rotations wipe months of compounded context: what the buyer cared about, the price commitments, the spec everyone agreed on.

The weekly loop, in manufacturing terms

What Monday, mid-week, and Friday actually look like in this sector. Same loop, written in your language.

◉ Monday brief

Top reorder accounts at slip risk. Top RFQs likely to close. The one channel-partner pattern that changed since Friday. The AM team gets their priorities by 9am.

◐ Mid-week priorities

Every AM walks into account calls with the last three touches, open RFQ threads, the spec still being debated, and the buyer-side question left unanswered.

✦ Friday recap

What moved across the book this week. Which RFQs progressed. Which channel partners came back. The plan for next Monday is already drafted.

Frequently asked

Our reorder cycles vary wildly by account. Can Nauti handle that?

Yes. Nauti reads each account's individual reorder pattern over months, not a one-size-fits-all rule. Monthly, quarterly, or stock-driven, the pattern is what matters and the pattern is what compounds in memory.

We sell through distributors and direct at the same time. Does that confuse it?

No. Nauti's account memory holds both shapes side by side. A direct account and a channel-partnered account look different in the data, and the brief reflects that. Each AM and channel manager gets a view scoped to their book.

Our deals involve engineering and procurement. Does Nauti understand spec discussions?

It reads them like any other buyer signal. A spec that keeps changing is a signal. A spec that gets locked is a signal. Engineering going quiet is a signal. The brief surfaces which ones matter this week and why.

Our CRM hygiene isn't great. Will Nauti still work?

Yes. Nauti reads email and chat alongside CRM and fills gaps. If the CRM is sparse but the inbox is rich, the inbox carries the memory. Improving CRM hygiene over time is a side-effect, not a precondition.

Who on our team uses it day-to-day?

Head of Sales gets the Monday brief and Friday recap. Each AM gets their own priority list and pre-call prep. The CRO gets the portfolio-level recap. Channel managers get partner-specific patterns. Same loop, different lens.
